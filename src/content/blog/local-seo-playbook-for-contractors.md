If you're a contractor and you're not showing up in the Google Map Pack when someone searches "HVAC repair near me" — you're invisible. Period.

46% of all Google searches have local intent. That means nearly half the people searching right now are looking for a business *in their area*. And the top 3 results in that Map Pack get **44% of all clicks**.

Here's how to get there.

## Step 1: Own Your Google Business Profile

This is the foundation. Everything else builds on this. If your Google Business Profile (GBP) is incomplete, outdated, or unoptimized, nothing else matters.

### The non-negotiables:

- **Complete every single field.** Business name, address, phone, hours, service area, categories, description. All of it.
- **Choose the right primary category.** "HVAC Contractor" is different from "Air Conditioning Repair Service." Pick the one that matches your highest-value service.
- **Add secondary categories.** You can have up to 10. Use them all if they're relevant.
- **Upload photos weekly.** Real photos of your work, your team, your trucks. Google rewards active profiles. Aim for 100+ total photos.
- **Post updates weekly.** New project completed? Special offer? Industry tip? Post it. This signals to Google that your business is active.

## Step 2: Get Reviews (Systematically)

Reviews are the single most important ranking factor for the Map Pack. Not just quantity — velocity and recency matter too.

### Build a review machine:

1. **Ask at the point of maximum satisfaction.** Right after the job is done and the customer is happy.
2. **Make it stupid easy.** Text them a direct link to your Google review page. One tap. That's it.
3. **Respond to every review.** Good and bad. Within 24 hours. Google watches this.
4. **Never incentivize reviews.** No discounts, no gifts. Google will catch you and the penalties are brutal.

Aim for 5+ new reviews per month at minimum. The top-ranking contractors in most markets have 200+ reviews.

## Step 3: Build Location-Specific Pages

This is where most contractors completely miss the boat. You have one "Services" page and wonder why you don't rank in any specific city.

**You need a page for every service in every city you serve.**

- "HVAC Repair in Cherry Hill, NJ"
- "Air Conditioning Installation in Marlton, NJ"
- "Furnace Replacement in Voorhees, NJ"

Each page should have:
- Unique, helpful content (not just the city name swapped out)
- Local landmarks, neighborhoods, and context
- Your service area map
- Reviews from customers in that area
- A clear call-to-action

Yes, this is a lot of pages. That's the point. Each one is a net catching searches your competitors aren't.

## Step 4: Nail Your On-Page SEO

Every page on your site needs these fundamentals:

- **Title tag:** Primary keyword + location + brand. "HVAC Repair in Cherry Hill, NJ | Lockard Heating & Air"
- **Meta description:** Compelling, includes keyword, has a call-to-action. Think of it as a mini-ad.
- **H1 tag:** One per page. Should closely match the title tag.
- **Internal links:** Link related service pages together. Link city pages to the parent service page.
- **Schema markup:** LocalBusiness schema at minimum. Service schema on service pages. FAQ schema on FAQ sections.

## Step 5: Citations and Directories

Your business name, address, and phone number (NAP) need to be consistent everywhere online. Inconsistencies confuse Google and hurt your rankings.

### Priority directories:
1. Google Business Profile
2. Bing Places
3. Apple Maps
4. Yelp
5. Facebook
6. BBB
7. Angi (formerly Angie's List)
8. HomeAdvisor
9. Thumbtack
10. Industry-specific directories

Use the exact same business name, address, and phone number on every single one. Character for character.

## Step 6: Content That Builds Authority

Google rewards businesses that demonstrate expertise. A blog isn't optional — it's your authority engine.

**Content ideas that work for contractors:**
- "How much does [service] cost in [city]?" (These rank like crazy)
- Seasonal maintenance guides
- Before/after project showcases
- "X signs you need [service]"
- Comparison guides (central air vs. mini-split, etc.)

Publish at least 2 quality posts per month. Each one is a new opportunity to rank for long-tail keywords your competitors ignore.

## The Timeline

Local SEO isn't overnight. Here's a realistic timeline:

- **Month 1-2:** Foundation work. GBP optimization, on-page SEO, citations.
- **Month 3-4:** Content production begins. Review velocity increasing. Initial ranking movement.
- **Month 5-6:** Noticeable ranking improvements. Phone starts ringing more.
- **Month 7-12:** Compounding returns. More reviews, more content, more authority. Competitors start wondering what happened.

The businesses that start now will be dominant in their market by year's end. The ones that wait will be playing catch-up.
